Of course. You should now have a new tab open in your web browser displaying the "Tradee Decision-Support System."

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to use the application we just built:

### Step 1: Select a Ticker
On the left side of the screen, you'll see a sidebar with a dropdown menu labeled **"Select a Ticker"**. Choose one of the stocks from the list (e.g., "RELIANCE.NS"). The main part of the page will automatically update with the analysis for that stock.

### Step 2: Analyze the Dashboard
Once the page loads, you will see several sections:
- **Market Regime:** This tells you the current market condition (e.g., "Trend Up," "Ranging").
- **Options Intelligence:** This shows you the key price levels (Call Wall and Put Wall) that might act as resistance or support.
- **Active Trade Monitor:** This is currently empty, but it's where your open trades will appear.

### Step 3: Plan a Mock Trade
Scroll down to the **"Trade Planner"** section. This is your main tool for creating and evaluating trades.
1.  Enter an **Entry Price**, **Stop-Loss Price**, and **Target Price** for a hypothetical trade.
2.  Make sure your **Portfolio Value** and **Risk per Trade (%)** are set to amounts you are comfortable with.
3.  Click the **"Calculate & Check Risk"** button.

### Step 4: Review and "Execute" the Trade
After you click the button, the app will display the results of your plan:
- It will show you the **Position Size** (how many shares to buy) and the **Risk/Reward Ratio**.
- It will then perform a **Pre-Trade Risk Check**. If the trade is within your rules, you will see a green "✅ TRADE APPROVED" message.
- An **"Execute Mock Trade"** button will appear. Click it.

### Step 5: Monitor Your Open Position
Now, look up at the **"Active Trade Monitor"** section. Your mock trade is now listed in the table!

Also, look at the **"Risk Manager Status"** in the sidebar. You will see that your **"Current Exposure"** has increased, because you now have an open position.

### Step 6: Set a Price Alert
Finally, let's test the alert system.
1.  In the sidebar, find the **"Alerts"** section.
2.  The ticker should already be filled in. Choose a **Condition** (e.g., "Rises Above") and set a **Target Price** that is close to the current price.
3.  Click **"Set Alert"**.

The alert is now active. If you were to refresh the page and the stock's price had crossed your target, a success message with balloons would appear at the top of the screen.
